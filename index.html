Secure Multi-Hop Relaying
in Space-Air-Ground-Sea Integrated Networks

Dataset demonstrations and appendices

(Abstract) We stand at the dawn of ubiquitous connectivity through 6G wireless, but its foundational reliability is threatened by the presence of invisible eavesdroppers (Eves). While the majority of research has relied on the unrealistic assumption of knowing Eve's position and channel state, this study tackles the challenge of secure multi-hop relaying without any Eve channel information. We (i) reformulate the notoriously intractable max-min secrecy-throughput problem into a tractable design, yielding an $\mathcal{O}(1)$ globally optimal radio-resource allocation; (ii) derive a closed-form expression of strictly positive secure connection probability under unknown Eve conditions and validate it across Rayleigh, Rician, and shadowed Rician fading; and (iii) construct the world's first open SAGSIN dataset, integrating SpaceX satellites, Google Loon HAPS, commercial 4G/5G base stations, and maritime vessels to bridge theory with real-world NTNs. This work pioneers a new physical-layer paradigm that guarantees security under uncertainty. It represents a game-changer for secure throughput-aware design in 6G non-terrestrial networks, laying the groundwork for the next era of resilient ubiquitous connectivity.

Dataset Usage Appendices

Dataset Usage

The SAGSIN dataset ships with tutorial.ipynb covering (1) a synthetic Natural-Earth sandbox, (2) the Madagascar–Mozambique channel (OpenCelliD + AIS + Sep. 2020 HAPS), and (3) a Western North America corridor that fuses Mexican and U.S. coastal infrastructure. Recreate every map by copying the lon/lat windows straight into env.DataManager.

Once a window is fixed, the loader fuses Starlink snapshots, HAPS trails, terrestrial cells, maritime tracks, and manual users automatically, so you can concentrate on secure-routing objectives instead of wrangling CSVs.

Use it like this:

import datetime as dt
from ssir import environment as env

# Hook directly into the open dataset
haps_coords = env.load_haps_positions(
    str(map_dir / "haps_positions.csv"),
    dt.datetime(2020, 9, 28, 10, 0),
)
