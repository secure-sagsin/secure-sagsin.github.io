Dataset Usage

How to explore the data

This prototype release highlights synthetic yet representative topologies spanning satellites, aerial relays, ground assets, and sea lanes. Use the accompanying notebooks to load the graphs, inspect hop metrics, and remix the routing costs for your own experiments. The pills below summarize the most common usage patterns.

Network Layers

Four intertwined theaters

Space

LEO assets form the backbone for long-haul hops, knitting distant theaters with predictable latency budgets.

Air

HAPS and UAV relays provide agile mid-tier routing, adapting to congestion while adding redundancy.

Ground

Dense terrestrial stations supply local breakouts, integrating with existing infrastructure and fiber egress points.

Sea

Maritime nodes close coverage gaps along shipping lanes and support over-the-horizon sensing missions.

Method

Secure multi-hop orchestration

Each hop is evaluated using a security-constrained metric that weighs signal-to-interference, relay trust scores, and physical path diversity. The routing engine blends deterministic solvers with learning-guided heuristics to remain scalable even as relay counts increase dramatically.

  • Adaptive metric aggregation (distance, secrecy, fairness)
  • Multi-objective pathfinding with policy fallbacks
  • Online updates from situational awareness feeds
